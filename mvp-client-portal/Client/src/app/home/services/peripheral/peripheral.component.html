Peripheral Arterial
Disease (PAD)

is a dangerous lack of blood flow through the limbs, usually the legs.

PAD is caused when arteries become too narrow and blood flow is reduced or blocked entirely. The most severe level of PAD is Critical Limb Ischemia (CLI).

Traditionally, especially in the geriatric population, CLI is treated with amputation. While amputation solves the CLI, patients are left with a new host of problems, including learning to navigate without the amputated limb.

Amputation is not the only option. MVP’s LEEP! program treats CLI — without losing any limbs.

What are the signs and
symptoms of Peripheral
Arterial Disease?

One of the most common symptoms of CLI is leg pain, called ischemic rest pain. The pain is most common when your legs are in a resting position or during your sleep

Other symptoms include:

  • Diabetic ulcers
  • Pain when walking
  • Long-lasting wounds
  • Excessive thickening of toenails
  • Lack of pulse in the limb
  • Dry gangrene
  • Extremely shiny and/or flaky skin on the foot or leg

The risk increases with age, but other factors can also contribute toward the chance of developing PAD.

  • Family history of atherosclerosis or claudication
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Inactive lifestyle
  • Obesity
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Chronic kidney disease
