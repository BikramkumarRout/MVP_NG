Understanding UFE

According to the NIH, each year, approximately 200,000 women undergo hysterectomies (removing all of the uterus) to alleviate symptoms caused by benign fibroid masses. Fibroid disease is behind nearly 60% of hysterectomies performed in the U.S. each year

But did you know that removing your uterus is not the only solution

MVP’s vascular specialists offer Uterine Fibroid Embolization (UFE), an alternative treatment that cures fibroids without a hysterectomy.

How do I know if I need UFE?

Many women with fibroids live with the pain and excessive menstrual bleeding, often missing work and family events. Are you experiencing these symptoms?

  • Extreme pain
  • Prolonged, heavy, painful periods
  • Frequent urination
  • Pain in the legs
  • Abdominal bloating
  • Backache
  • Reduced sexual desire or function

Non-invasive procedure

No long-term side effects. Just an effective treatment.

  • 1 During the simple UFE procedure, surgeons block the artery supplying blood to the fibroid, which shrinks the fibroid and resolves the symptoms
  • 2 The procedure is conducted through one tiny skin nick, requiring no stitches, reducing pain and recovery time for the patient. Patients are typically in-and-out of the procedure room in under an hour
