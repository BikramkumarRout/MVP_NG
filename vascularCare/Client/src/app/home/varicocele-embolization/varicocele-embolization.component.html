10% of men have varicoceles

(varicose veins
in the scrotum).

Varicoceles are small bulges of blood that can't flow properly. The lack of blood flow is usually caused when the valves in the scrotal veins malfunction

One-Way Valve (good blood flow)
Two-Way Valve (bad blood flow)

Varicoceles are most prevalent in men ages 15-35. In extreme cases, varicoceles can lead to:

  • Pain
  • Infertility
  • Testicular atrophy (shrinkage)

If you have been diagnosed with a varicocele, varicocele embolization is a highly-effective, minimally-invasive treatment that can help.

MVP’s varicocele embolization has a success rate of 90%, which is the same as surgical techniques — without requiring surgery

Varicocele
Embolization

Varicocele embolization is a highly effective, minimally-invasive alternative to surgery, where a vascular specialist closes blood flow to the affected veins, causing the varicocele to shrink and die off.

Our physicians at MVP can perform your procedure at any of our convenient locations. The vascular specialist and clinical staff at our outpatient vascular centers combine medical expertise and compassion to guide you through treatment, getting you back to your daily routine quickly.

Benefits

  • Performed as an outpatient procedure, requiring no overnight hospital stay
  • Requires no general anesthesia
  • Utilizes only a small nick that does not require stitches — and results in no scarring
  • Quicker recovery than in-patient surgical procedures (days instead of weeks)
  • Reduced risk of infection compared to surgery

The Minimally-Invasive,
Quick-Recovery Procedure

  • 1 You will be given a mild sedative to help you relax and a local anesthetic at the procedure site.
  • 2 The vascular specialist will use fluoroscopy (a type of x-ray, image-guided technology) to insert a catheter through a small nick in your groin.
  • 3The catheter will be placed through your femoral vein and advanced into the affected testicular vein.
  • 4 Contrast (a type of dye) is injected to help your physician visualize your veins so they know exactly where to embolize (block) the varicocele.
  • 5 Your physician places small coils and/or sclerosants, a medicine, to embolize the blood flow.
  • 6 Once the vein is blocked, the varicocele will no longer be able to live.
  • 7 You’ll begin to feel relief within days.
