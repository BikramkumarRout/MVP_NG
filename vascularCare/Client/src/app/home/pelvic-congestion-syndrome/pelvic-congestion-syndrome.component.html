According to the National Institute of Health, a staggering

10 million women suffer from Pelvic Congestion Syndrome (PCS).

PCC (or pelvic vein incompetence) is a chronic medical condition where varicose veins develop in the lower abdomen.

The condition causes debilitating pain, and when left untreated, PDF long-term disability, depression and neurological changes.

Ovarian Vein Embolization is a non-invasive, out-patient procedure that seals the affected veins, relieving pressure and the resulting symptoms.

PCS is most common for women ages 20-45.

Condition and Cause

Here are several factors, including:

  • Multiple pregnancies
  • Hormonal changes
  • Weight gain
  • Presence of a “tipped” uterus
  • Polycystic ovaries

Symptoms

Those who suffer from PCS deal with a host of symptoms.

  • Dull aches, sharp or throbbing pain in the pelvis or lower back
  • Pain while or after sitting or standing for a long time, which is relieved by lying down
  • Irritable bladder that sometimes lead to unintentional urination
  • Irritable bowel that sometimes leads to recurrent abdominal pain and diarrhea alternating with periods of constipation
  • Pain during or after sexual intercourse
  • Vaginal varicose veins
  • Varicose veins on inner thighs or the back thighs
  • Lower back pain or achy legs
  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding

Diagnosis

In order to diagnose PCS, our skilled clinicians will use one of the following diagnostic techniques to identify which veins are affected.

  • Ultrasound
  • CT Scanning or MRI
  • Pelvic Venography

Treatment

We offer a minimally invasive and non-surgical treatment for PCS called Ovarian Vein Embolization. Ovarian Vein Embolization is performed as an outpatient procedure with no overnight hospital stay.

Using ultrasound guidance, our skilled interventional radiologists insert a tiny catheter with a sclerosing agent, which seals the affected vein and relieves the pain and pressure. The entire procedure takes only thirty minutes.

The cost of this treatment is covered by most insurance plans. We have a dedicated insurance department to help you with your insurance related questions.

Recovery

Immediately after the procedure, some women experience pelvic cramping, which only lasts a few days. most women comfortably return to work after a few weeks.

According to radiologyinfo.org the success rate for ovarian vein embolization is 85%. You will finally experience relief from the painful symptoms that had been plaguing you.
